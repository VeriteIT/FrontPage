TAMIL PRESS PRIORITIES
Photo credits: Jaffna Monitor

Trincomalee Buddha statue issue; Sajith must apologise to Tamil people for his statement – MP Shritharan urges

Virakesari, Thinakkural, Thinakaran, Thamilan

ITAK MP S. Shritharan asserted in Parliament that Leader of the Opposition Sajith Premadasa must withdraw his statement regarding the Trincomalee Buddha statue controversy and publicly apologise to the Tamil people. Speaking in Parliament, Shritharan stated that the Tamil people voted for Premadasa in the presidential election believing all their problems would be solved, and accused him of betraying those same people. He noted that the statue’s installation has caused anger among the Tamil people. He claimed that the controversy is a result of continuous Sinhala settlements and that the current incident has manifested as a racial spectre. Shritharan compared the situation to the politics of former President J.R. Jayewardene, who used Buddhist monks to act against the Tamil people, suggesting that the current government may be operating in a similar manner.

"Everyone’s advice is needed for the Provincial Council election system; there is no plan to postpone it" – Minister Bimal

Thinakkural, Thamilan, Virakesari

Minister and Leader of the House Bimal Rathnayake stated in Parliament that, when creating a new electoral system to conduct the Provincial Council election, advice should be sought from all parties. He emphasised that this process is not intended to postpone the election. The minister, responding to a question from Chief Opposition Whip MP Gayantha Karunathilaka, pointed out that the previous ‘Good Governance’ government introduced a mixed electoral system (50% ward-based and 50% list-based), replacing the old system. However, he stated that the main current obstacle to holding an election under the new system is the delimitation process, which lacks technical aspects and must be resolved. He concluded that consulting with all parties would be a democratic step.

"Let’s all collectively review the necessity of the Provincial Council system" – MP Faiszer Musthapha

Thinakkural, Thinakaran

MP Faiszer Musthapha stated in Parliament that it is necessary for everyone to collectively consider and review the need for the Provincial Council system. He asked what negative consequences had occurred since the Provincial Council elections have not been held for eleven years. He questioned whether provincial development had been lacking, suggesting that this required a review of the Provincial Council elections. Musthapha noted that eleven years covers two election cycles and, while he stressed he is not calling for a delay, urged that all should jointly examine the success of the system. He said he is often scapegoated for the failure to hold the election and called this an injustice, noting that no president—from Maithripala Sirisena (under whose tenure the new system was introduced) to Ranil Wickremesinghe and Gotabaya Rajapaksa—held the elections.

"Federalism is needed for Eelam Tamils; this is the time for India to intervene" – Thirumavalavan urges

Thinakkural

Indian parliamentarian Thol. Thirumavalavan sharply criticised the Sri Lankan government for allowing the erection of a Buddha statue in Trincomalee, warning that the episode exposes the NPP administration as another Sinhala supremacist government. He argued that the incident demonstrates the JVP-led government’s alignment with the longstanding Sinhala Buddhist chauvinist agenda, actively facilitating a systematic cultural intrusion and expansion in the Tamil homeland. Noting the NPP’s plan to draft a new constitution, Thirumavalavan cautioned that this process will not address the structural inequalities faced by Eelam Tamils unless the Government of India intervenes. He called upon India to play a meaningful role in securing a new constitutional settlement that includes a genuinely federal model to protect the rights and security of the Tamil people.

SINHALA PRESS PRIORITIES
Photo credits: Daily News

A planned murder spree at the same location in Midigama narrowly prevented

Divaina, Lankadeepa, Mawbima

The Western Province North Crimes Division arrested a former army soldier for allegedly plotting to assassinate a hotel owner and his family members in the Midigama area. The 35-year-old suspect, a resident of Tangalle, was apprehended with a T-56 assault rifle, a .38 revolver, ammunition, and a hand grenade. Police investigations revealed that the contract was orchestrated by Dubai-based organised crime figure Army Chuti due to a dispute over extortion or business rivalry involving the incarcerated underworld leader Nadun Chinthaka, alias Harak Kata. Police stated that further investigations are underway to apprehend accomplices who fled the scene.

The secrets of the underworld: shots fired in a shop in Unakuruwa | The motorcycle used for the murder of the couple in Unakuruwa found

Aruna, Divaina, Mawbima

Investigations have commenced into the killing of a couple who were shot dead inside their grocery shop in Unakuruwa, Tangalle, on November 18. The victims have been identified as the uncle and aunt of Dubai-based organised crime figure known as Unakuruwe Shantha. Investigations suggest the double murder was carried out as an act of revenge orchestrated by rival underworld leader Karandeniye Sudda, reportedly in retaliation for a previous attack on his brother-in-law. Police later discovered the motorcycle used by the suspects abandoned in Seenimodara. Four special police teams have been deployed to investigate the incident, which is believed to be related to an ongoing power struggle between criminal gangs.

“The rally in Nugegoda will be held on the 21st, regardless of any obstacles” – Joint opposition party leaders

Lankadeepa

Leaders of the joint opposition announced that the mass rally organised by several opposition parties under the theme, “The great public outcry against the NPP government’s foolish rule” will definitely be held in Nugegoda on November 21. Speaking at a press conference in Colombo yesterday (19th), party representatives stated that the protest focuses on three main demands: fulfilling promises made by the government, ending corruption within the government, and ending government-led terror. Pivithuru Hela Urumaya (PHU) Leader Udaya Gammanpila warned that the government might attempt to sabotage the event using false propaganda or AI technology, but confirmed the rally would go ahead despite any obstacles. In addition, SLPP General Secretary Sagara Kariyawasam claimed this would be the largest anti-government protest in history. UNP Deputy Secretary General of Political Mobilisation Harin Fernando criticised the current administration for its lack of direction and defended the efforts of former Presidents Ranil Wickremesinghe and Mahinda Rajapaksa to collaborate and unite other opposition groups.

"A new political solution is essential for the problems faced by the people of the North and East" – President states at the meeting with ITAK members

Dinamina, Divaina, Mawbima, Thinakkural, Virakesari, Thamilan

A meeting was held between President Anura Kumara Dissanayake and a delegation from the Illankai Tamil Arasu Kadchi (ITAK) yesterday (19th) at the Presidential Secretariat, following a request from the party. Discussions centred on the Provincial Council elections and the need for a new constitution. The president emphasised that the government believes the longstanding issues faced by the people of the Northern and Eastern Provinces can no longer be resolved through outdated political solutions. Therefore, it is essential to pursue a new political solution with the cooperation of all stakeholders. The ITAK raised longstanding regional issues such as fisheries disputes, land concerns, and the need for improved infrastructure and development in the North and East. Tamil press report: ITAK General Secretary M.A. Sumanthiran stated that they emphasised to the president the need to quickly fulfil his election promise regarding the political solution, noting that a year had passed with no progress. The president agreed to start related actions in January. He added that they provided the president with a copy of the final draft of the solution from the Steering Committee, which contains sixteen principles on power devolution, with all but two agreed upon by everyone. The representatives pressed for a solution based on a federal structure, insisting that devolved powers must not be withdrawn; the president agreed to this, Sumanthiran noted.

“Solve the Trincomalee temple problem peacefully” – The Court of Appeal proposes

Aruna, Divaina, Lankadeepa, Mawbima, Thinakaran, Thamilan, Virakesari

The Court of Appeal has directed the Department of Coast Conservation and Coastal Resource Management to reinspect the premises of the Sri Sambuddha Jayanthi Bodhiraja Viharaya in Trincomalee regarding an order to demolish part of the temple. This directive follows a writ petition filed by Chief Incumbent Ven. Thrikunamalaye Kalyanavansa Tissa Thera challenging the department’s decision to remove alleged unauthorised structures on the temple premises. Representing the Department of Coast Conservation and Coastal Resource Management, Deputy Solicitor General Manohara Jayasinghe argued that while an approved plan exists, the new constructions exceed its boundaries and are illegal under the Coast Conservation Act. Justices Dhammika Ganepola and Adithya Patabendige stressed the importance of a peaceful resolution to avoid potential unrest and scheduled the next hearing for December 16, at which the progress of the inspection will be reported to court.

“A case will be filed in a higher court once investigations are completed within a month” – The AG informs the court

Aruna, Dinamina, Divaina, Lankadeepa, Mawbima, Virakesari

Appearing for the Attorney General, Additional Solicitor General (ASG) Dileepa Peiris informed Colombo Magistrate’s Court yesterday (19th) that investigations into former President Ranil Wickremesinghe regarding the alleged misuse of state funds will be completed within a month. The ASG explained that the CID has finished nearly 90% of the inquiry into the expenditure of over Rs. 16.6 million for an alleged private visit to the UK by the former president to attend his spouse’s graduation. Consequently, the prosecution plans to file indictments in a higher court.
