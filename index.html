Trincomalee Buddha statue issue; Sajith must apologise to Tamils for his statement – MP Sridharan urges

Virakesari, Thinakkural, Thinakaran, Thamilan

ITAK MP S. Shritharan stated in Parliament that Leader of the Opposition Sajith Premadasa must withdraw his remarks regarding the Trincomalee Buddha statue controversy and publicly apologise to the Tamil people. Speaking in Parliament, Shritharan said the Tamil community voted for Premadasa in the presidential election in the belief that all their problems would be resolved, but he has since betrayed them. He pointed out that the enshrinement of the statue has angered the Tamil people, alleging that this controversy stems from ongoing Sinhala settlements and has manifested as a racial provocation. Shritharan likened the situation to the politics of former President J.R. Jayewardene, who allegedly used Buddhist monks against the Tamil people, suggesting that the current government may be acting similarly.

Everyone's advice is needed for the Provincial Council election system; there is no plan to postpone it – Minister Bimal

Thinakkural, Thamilan, Virakesari

Minister and Leader of the House Bimal Rathnayake stated in Parliament that when designing a new electoral system for the Provincial Council election, the advice of all parties should be sought. He emphasised that this process is not intended to postpone the election. Responding to a question from Chief Opposition Whip MP Gayantha Karunathilaka, the Minister recalled that the previous "Good Governance" government introduced a mixed electoral system (50% ward–based and 50% list–based), replacing the old system. He pointed out that the main obstacle to holding an election under the new system is the delimitation process, which is technically incomplete and needs to be addressed. He concluded that consulting all parties would be a democratic approach.

Let's all collectively review the necessity of the Provincial Council system – MP Faiszer Musthapha

Thinakkural, Thinakaran

MP Faiszer Musthapha stated in Parliament that everyone should come together to review the need for the Provincial Council system. He questioned what negative impact had occurred during the eleven years in which Provincial Council elections were not held, asking whether development in these provinces had lagged. He said this suggests a review of the Provincial Council elections is necessary. Musthapha observed that eleven years is equivalent to two election cycles and, although not calling for further delays, urged that the effectiveness of the system be jointly examined. He added that he is often held responsible for the failure to hold the election, which he considers unfair, as no president—from Maithripala Sirisena (who introduced the new system) to Ranil Wickremesinghe and Gotabaya Rajapaksa—held the polls.

Federalism is needed for Eelam Tamils; this is the time for India to intervene – Thirumavalavan urges

Thinakkural

Indian parliamentarian Thol. Thirumavalavan strongly criticised the Sri Lankan government for permitting the erection of a Buddha statue in Trincomalee, warning that the episode exposes the NPP administration as another Sinhala supremacist government. He argued that the incident reveals the JVP–led government's alignment with longstanding Sinhala Buddhist chauvinist policies, actively facilitating a programme of cultural expansionism in the Tamil homeland. Noting the NPP’s plans to draft a new Constitution, Thirumavalavan cautioned that this process will not address the structural inequalities faced by Eelam Tamils unless the Government of India intervenes. He urged India to play a meaningful role in securing a new constitutional arrangement with a genuinely federal structure protecting the rights and security of the Tamil people.

A cluster of murders set to occur at the same location in Midigama narrowly prevented

Divaina, Lankadeepa, Mawbima

The Western Province North Crimes Division has arrested a former army soldier on suspicion of plotting to assassinate a hotel owner and his family in the Midigama area. The thirty–five–year–old suspect, a resident of Tangalle, was apprehended with a T–56 assault rifle, a .38 revolver, ammunition and a hand grenade. Police investigations revealed that the contract was allegedly arranged by Dubai–based organised crime figure Army Chuti, due to a dispute involving extortion or business rivalry with the incarcerated underworld leader Nadun Chinthaka, alias Harak Kata. Police said further investigations are underway to track down accomplices who fled the scene.

Secrets of the underworld: shot fired in a shop in Unakuruwa | The motorcycle used in the murder of the couple in Unakuruwa found

Aruna, Divaina, Mawbima

Investigations have been launched into the deaths of a couple, who were shot inside their grocery store in Unakuruwa, Tangalle, on November 18. The victims were the uncle and aunt of Dubai–based organised crime figure Unakuruwe Shantha. Police believe the double murder was an act of revenge orchestrated by rival underworld leader Karandeniye Sudda, allegedly in retaliation for a previous attack on his brother–in–law. Police later discovered the suspects' motorcycle abandoned in the Seenimodara area. Four special police teams have been deployed in connection with the incident, which is believed to be part of an ongoing gang power struggle.

We will hold the rally in Nugegoda on the 21st, regardless of any obstacles – Joint opposition party leaders

Lankadeepa

Leaders of the joint opposition have confirmed that a mass rally, organised by several opposition parties under the theme "The great public outcry against the NPP government's foolish rule", will take place in Nugegoda on November 21. At a media briefing in Colombo yesterday (19th), party representatives stated that the protest would focus on three main demands: the government must honour its promises, end corruption, and stop its campaign of oppression. Pivithuru Hela Urumaya (PHU) Leader Udaya Gammanpila warned that the government might attempt to sabotage the event with false propaganda or AI technology, but insisted the rally would go ahead regardless. SLPP General Secretary Sagara Kariyawasam claimed it would be the largest anti–government rally ever. UNP Deputy Secretary General for Political Mobilisation Harin Fernando criticised the current administration for its lack of direction and defended steps taken by former presidents Ranil Wickremesinghe and Mahinda Rajapaksa to unite the opposition.

A new political solution is essential for the problems faced by the people of the North and East – President states at the meeting with ITAK members

Dinamina, Divaina, Mawbima, Thinakkural, Virakesari, Thamilan

A meeting was held yesterday (19th) at the Presidential Secretariat between President Anura Kumara Dissanayake and a delegation of Illankai Tamil Arasu Kadchi (ITAK) members, following a request from the party. Discussions focused on the Provincial Council elections and the need for a new Constitution. The President emphasised that the government believes the long–standing issues faced by people in the Northern and Eastern Provinces can no longer be addressed through outdated political solutions. Therefore, it is necessary to advance a new political solution with the involvement of all stakeholders. The ITAK delegation raised long–standing regional issues such as fisheries disputes, land concerns and the need for improved infrastructure and development in the North and East. In the Tamil press report: ITAK General Secretary M.A. Sumanthiran said they stressed the need for the President to fulfil his election promise of a political solution urgently, noting that a year had passed without progress. The President agreed to begin the necessary actions in January. Sumanthiran added that they presented the President with the final draft proposal from the Steering Committee, containing sixteen power devolution principles, all but two of which had been broadly agreed. The delegation advocated a solution based on federalism, insisting devolved powers must not be withdrawn, to which the President assented, Sumanthiran noted.

Resolve the problem of the Trincomalee temple peacefully – The Court of Appeal proposes

Aruna, Divaina, Lankadeepa, Mawbima, Thinakaran, Thamilan, Virakesari

The Court of Appeal has directed the Department of Coast Conservation and Coastal Resource Management to re–inspect the Sri Sambuddha Jayanthi Bodhiraja Viharaya premises in Trincomalee in relation to an order to demolish part of the temple. This follows a writ petition by Chief Incumbent Ven. Thrikunamalaye Kalyana Wansha Tissa Thera, who is challenging the department’s decision to remove allegedly unauthorised structures from the site. Appearing for the department, Deputy Solicitor General Manohara Jayasinghe argued that while an approved plan exists, the new constructions go beyond these limits and are illegal under the Coast Conservation Act. Justices Dhammika Ganepola and Adithya Patabendige stressed the importance of a peaceful solution to prevent unrest, and scheduled the case for December 16, when the court will be updated on the inspection’s progress.

A case to be filed in a higher court after investigations are concluded within a month – The Attorney General informs the court

Aruna, Dinamina, Divaina, Lankadeepa, Mawbima, Virakesari

Additional Solicitor General (ASG) Dileepa Peiris, appearing for the Attorney General, informed the Colombo Magistrate’s Court yesterday (19th) that the investigation into former president Ranil Wickremesinghe regarding the alleged misuse of state funds will be completed within a month. The ASG stated that the CID has concluded nearly 90% of the inquiry into the expenditure of over Rs. 16.6 million, allegedly for a private visit to the UK by the former president to attend his spouse’s graduation. The prosecution therefore intends to file indictments in a higher court.
