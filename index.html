TAMIL PRESS PRIORITIES
Photo credits: Lanka Sara

"Tamil people despise the Ministry of Defence, which is responsible for all their suffering" – Gajendrakumar Ponnambalam

Thinakkural, Virakesari, Thamilan

All Ceylon Tamil Congress (ACTC) MP Gajendrakumar Ponnambalam stated that Tamil people strongly resent the Ministry of Defence, which he asserts is responsible for all the suffering endured by Tamils during and after the civil war. Speaking in parliament yesterday (18th), he alleged that military personnel in the North and East are involved in drug distribution, and that the drug issue worsened after the war, unlike during the LTTE administration. Ponnambalam demanded that the government acknowledge war crimes and human rights abuses perpetrated during the conflict, citing evidence released by credible members within the military. He insisted that as the government and military face such allegations, they must agree to international investigations to gain the trust of the Tamil community.

"Will Tamils ever get justice in Sri Lanka? The Anura government’s true nature exposed today; Buddhist monks are the country’s executive presidents" – Jaffna University Students’ Union strongly condemns

Virakesari, Thinakkural

Representatives of the Jaffna University Students’ Union argued that the underlying cause of the ethnic conflict and war is Sinhala Buddhist majoritarianism, which has led to Tamil lands being continually subjected to Buddhist-Sinhala dominance under the guise of Buddha worship. At a media briefing yesterday (18th), they said that Tamils, who had hoped for a non-racist political culture by voting for change, have been completely let down by the illegal re-installation of the statue in Trincomalee. The union questioned why the law has not been enforced against those who illegally installed the statue, disturbed the peace, and assaulted the police. They warned that they would reject the systematic land grabs carried out under the pretext of worship, concluding that the state apparatus is entirely subservient to the monks.

“Is placing Buddha’s statues in Lord Shiva’s premises an act of reconciliation?” – Premachandran questions

Virakesari

Eelam People’s Revolutionary Liberation Front (EPRLF) Leader and District Tamil National Alliance (DTNA) Spokesperson Suresh Premachandran stated that the illegal erection of Buddha statues and ongoing Buddhisation in Trincomalee and the Koneswaram Temple area, undermining its historical and religious significance for Tamils, will not promote the country’s development or ethnic harmony. He noted that successive Sinhala governments have surrounded the Koneswaram Temple—a sacred site mentioned in the Devaarams (Hindu devotional poems) and over 2,500 years old—with Buddha statues and Buddhist institutions. Premachandran pointed out that sites traditionally used by Tamils for rites, such as the Kannya Hot Water Wells, have been taken over by Buddhist monks who now manage the area and are demolishing Hindu temples there.

"Do not release land held by the military! National security is paramount," – SJB MP Nalin Bandara insists in parliament

Thinakkural, Thamilan

SJB MP Nalin Bandara urged the government not to return lands linked to national security in the Northern Province, stressing that national security must override individual concerns in land release decisions. Speaking in parliament yesterday (18th), he insisted that the 30-year war is not entirely over and that future conflicts are possible, necessitating the retention of strategically vital land by the military. Bandara accused the government of acting irresponsibly and neglecting national security to win votes in the North. He suggested that, rather than returning security-related lands, the government should offer alternative land or full compensation to the rightful owners.

"No ban on commemorating relatives; if the LTTE is commemorated, legal action will follow” – Nalinda Jayatissa

Virakesari

Cabinet Spokesperson Nalinda Jayatissa stated that there are no restrictions on citizens commemorating their relatives who died during the country’s 30-year civil war, and that every citizen has this right. However, he made it clear that if terrorists are commemorated under this pretext, legal action will be instituted. Minister Jayatissa also indicated that since the government took office in November last year, various factions have tried to provoke racism, but these attempts have failed. He confirmed that the government is committed to preventing such situations from recurring and will take all necessary steps to promote ethnic unity and reconciliation, ensuring peace and stability in the country.

SINHALA PRESS PRIORITIES
Photo credits: Sri Lanka Mirror

NPP takes control of the Seethawaka Pradeshiya Sabha six months after the election

Lankadeepa, Divaina, Mawbima, Dinamina

Six months after the Local Government (LG) Election, the NPP assumed power in the Seethawaka Pradeshiya Sabha yesterday (18th). P.K. Premarathna, an NPP member, was appointed as Chairperson of the Seethawaka Pradeshiya Sabha. In the previous LG Election, no party or independent group secured more than 50% of the seats needed to form a majority. The NPP won 23 seats, while the other 24 went to various opposition parties. As a result, the Seethawaka Pradeshiya Sabha was unable to form an administration until yesterday’s session (18th). Initially, it was determined whether the chairperson’s vote would be by open or secret ballot. The 23 NPP members and one Sarvajana Balaya representative from the opposition supported a secret ballot. Subsequently, a vote was held, resulting in NPP’s Premarathna being elected Chairperson.

“None of the government bungalows have been allocated to MPs or ministers” – Nalinda Jayatissa

Dinamina

Cabinet Spokesperson Minister Nalinda Jayatissa said that no government bungalows or houses have been provided to any MP, minister, or politician. He made this statement yesterday (18th) during the Cabinet decisions media briefing at the Department of Government Information. Minister Jayatissa added that, of the 32 government bungalows, six have been temporarily leased: four to the Ministry of Justice, one to the Police Department, and one to the Commission to Investigate Allegations of Bribery or Corruption (CIABOC).

Government programme to address issues facing local potato farmers

Dinamina

The government plans to implement a systematic programme through short-, medium- and long-term initiatives to address the problems faced by local potato farmers. The programme will include buying potatoes from farmers at Rs. 200 to Rs. 300 per kilogram, introducing a purchasing mechanism in partnership with Sathosa, and raising the tax on imported potatoes. A meeting about these measures was held yesterday (18th) at the Presidential Secretariat with Minister of Agriculture, Livestock, Land and Irrigation K.D. Lalkantha, Minister of Trade, Commerce, Food Security and Cooperative Development Wasantha Samarasinghe, and local potato farmers.

“I will not speak in parliament for one month if 20,000 people do not attend the Nugegoda rally” – MP Chamara Sampath

Lankadeepa

New Democratic Front (NDF) MP Chamara Sampath declared that he would remain silent in parliament for one month unless 20,000 people attend the planned anti-government rally in Nugegoda on November 21. The rally is being organised by the united opposition. Sampath made this statement in parliament yesterday (18th).

Trincomalee statue incident: President requests report from the Secretary of Public Security | "No tolerance for racism: future murder investigations, including Thajudeen’s, will be thorough" – President

Lankadeepa, Divaina, Mawbima, Dinamina, Aruna, Virakesari, Thinakaran, Thinakkural, Thamilan

President Anura Kumara Dissanayake told parliament yesterday (18th) that he has directed the Secretary to the Ministry of Public Security to submit a report on the incident at the Sri Sambuddha Jayanthi Bodhiraja Viharaya in Trincomalee. President Dissanayake also pledged to eradicate the drug trade and organised criminal gangs, vowing to create a clean state for future generations by rooting out conspiracies. He assured that investigations into serious crimes, such as the deaths of Thajudeen and the Easter Sunday attacks, would be conducted thoroughly going forward. The president stressed that the aim of undermined forces is to exploit opportunities for racism, and he promised that no space would be given for racism to arise again. He made these remarks during debates on the expenditure for the Ministry of Defence and the Ministry of Public Security and Parliamentary Affairs.

Draft approved: Bill to repeal pensions for 330 former MPs to be tabled in parliament soon

Lankadeepa, Divaina, Mawbima, Dinamina, Virakesari, Thinakaran

The Cabinet has approved the submission of the Parliamentary Pensions Law (Repeal) Bill to parliament. Cabinet Spokesperson Minister Nalinda Jayatissa said that once the Bill is passed, MPs will no longer be entitled to a parliamentary pension. The minister made this announcement yesterday (18th) during a Cabinet decisions media briefing at the Department of Government Information.
