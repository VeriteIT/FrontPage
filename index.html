“Allocate at least Rs.1.1 billion to conduct the election” – Weerawansa

Thamilan, Thinakkural

Freedom People’s Alliance (FPA) Leader Wimal Weerawansa stated that the president cannot stage a drama in parliament and must limit his actions to his secretariat. He further insisted that measures should be taken to release the Rs.1.1 billion the Election Commission claims is required to conduct the election. Otherwise, he warned, the government is likely to face serious opposition and consequences, as in previous incidents.

Sri Lankan High Commission surrounded in Chennai

Thinakkural

The May 17 movement of Tamil Nadu organised a protest in front of the Sri Lankan High Commission, condemning what they say were severe attacks by the Sri Lanka Navy on Indian fisherfolk from Tharangambadi and Karaikal over the past two weeks. Thirumurugan Gandhi, coordinator of the May 17 movement, demanded that Sri Lanka cease the violence against Tamil Nadu’s fisherfolk.

“Ten new ministers to be sworn in” – Dissanayake

Thinakkural, Thamilan

SLPP MP S.B. Dissanayake stated that President Wickremesinghe will soon appoint ten new ministers, including himself. He added that Gamini Lokuge, Janaka Bandara Thennakoon, Johnston Fernando, S.M. Chandrasena, C.B. Rathnayake, Rohitha Abegunawardhana, and Sarath Weerasekera will also be sworn in as ministers at the upcoming ceremony.

No political interference in TRC – HRCSL tells president

Virakesari

The Human Rights Commission of Sri Lanka (HRCSL) has recommended to the president that the Truth and Reconciliation Commission (TRC), established by parliamentary statute, must be guaranteed independence. The HRCSL stated that the TRC should be empowered to uncover facts, promote reconciliation, and establish peace and justice without any political interference.

SINHALA PRESS PRIORITIES
Photo credits:

CCTV cameras mandatory for school vans

Dinamina, Aruna

Minister of Transport, Highways, and Urban Development Bimal Rathnayake stated yesterday (24th) in parliament that installation of CCTV cameras in school vans will be compulsory from next January. Rathnayake made the statement during the debate on the ministry’s budget allocations. He added that this measure will allow parents to monitor their children from home. Rathnayake also stated: "Instructions will be issued for school vans and office vans from January. Passengers should be made aware that harassment of women or children in public transport will not be tolerated. If something wrong occurs and no one speaks out, that is not appropriate. People should not pretend not to see wrongdoing." He continued that steps would be taken to professionalise the bus and three-wheeler industries.

22,000 kg of drugs seized in eleven months: 26,573 arrested in twenty-five days through Ratama Ekata operation

Dinamina

Over the twenty-five days since the launch of the "Ratama Ekata" (A Nation United) operation on October 30 up to Sunday (23rd), 26,573 people have been arrested in raids seizing nearly 1,850 kilogrammes of illicit drugs. The largest quantity was found during raids at sea. The Sri Lanka Navy (SLN) conducted these operations based on intelligence from the Police Narcotics Bureau (PNB), State Intelligence Service, and Navy Intelligence Unit. This is reported to be the largest quantity of drugs seized within such a short period in Sri Lankan history. Including this haul, between January 1 and now, 221,246 people have been arrested and 22,056 kilogrammes of drugs seized in nationwide raids.

UNP and SJB set for talks on possible alliance

Lankadeepa

General Secretary of the SJB Ranjith Madduma Bandara stated that SJB Leader Sajith Premadasa has agreed to work with the UNP. He said both parties will soon hold high-level discussions. The General Secretary further stated that all arrangements between the two parties will be made with the knowledge of SJB Leader Sajith Premadasa.

Six buried alive: landslide in previously issued warning zone; four hospitalised

Lankadeepa, Divaina, Mawbima, Dinamina, Aruna, Virakesari, Thinakaran, Thinakkural, Thamilan

On Saturday (22nd) morning, a landslide with a large rock struck a house and a shop in the Lower Kadugannawa area on the Colombo–Kandy main road. The death toll from the incident, which occurred around 8.45 am, has risen to six. The bodies of four victims were initially found, and the remaining two, who were trapped under debris, were recovered yesterday (23rd). Five people injured in the accident are being treated in hospital. The accident happened when a large rock and several smaller rocks below the railway line fell onto the house and shop. A rescue operation was carried out by police, the army, residents, and health workers to assist those trapped inside the shop. A section of the Colombo–Kandy road remains closed following the landslide. Newspapers reported that warnings had been issued about the building due to adverse weather conditions.

Politically influential figures remove archaeological site signboards in Batticaloa: legal action to follow – Director General of Archaeology

Mawbima, Aruna, Thinakkural, Thamilan

Police have launched an investigation after receiving complaints yesterday (23rd) regarding the removal of five signboards installed at archaeological sites in the Manmunai South and Koralaipattu Valaichchenai Divisional Secretariat areas in Batticaloa. The boards, identifying sites such as Thandamalai (Rahat Gala), Murunkantivu, Thikkilivettai, Pendukkal Chenai, and Saraveli, were removed, loaded onto tractors, and taken away. Acting Director General of the Department of Archaeology Thusitha Ranasinghe said complaints have been made and legal action will be taken. Archaeological signboards recently erected by the Department of Archaeology in villages within the Koralaipattu Divisional Council area were removed on November 22 by a group including the Divisional Council Chairperson, members, and local residents. The signs were placed at junctions on roads in areas including Kudumbimalai and surrounding hills, ancient temples, and farmlands in the Kiran area. Divisional Council Chairperson S. Sudhakaran claimed the signs were installed without council approval and accused the Department of Archaeology of neglecting the local community’s language. He also alleged that the signs were intended to bring those areas under the department’s jurisdiction in the future.
