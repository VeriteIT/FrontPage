“Tamil people hate the Ministry of Defence, which is responsible for all their suffering” – Gajendrakumar Ponnambalam

Thinakkural, Virakesari, Thamilan

All Ceylon Tamil Congress (ACTC) MP Gajendrakumar Ponnambalam asserted that the Tamil people deeply resent the Ministry of Defence, which he claimed is responsible for the suffering experienced by Tamils during and after the civil war. Speaking in Parliament yesterday (18th), he alleged that military personnel in the North and East are involved in drug distribution, noting that the drug issue escalated after the war, unlike during the LTTE era. Ponnambalam demanded that the government must acknowledge the war crimes and human rights violations that took place during the conflict, referencing evidence released by credible individuals within the military. He insisted that as the government and military face these accusations, they must agree to international investigations to rebuild trust with the Tamil community.

“Will Tamils ever get justice in Sri Lanka? The Anura government’s true face exposed today; Buddhist monks are the country’s executive presidents” – Jaffna University Students’ Union strongly condemns

Virakesari, Thinakkural

Representatives of the Jaffna University Students’ Union stated that the root cause of the ethnic conflict and the war is Sinhala Buddhist majoritarianism, resulting in continued encroachment on Tamil lands under the guise of Buddha worship. Speaking at a media briefing yesterday (18th), they argued that the Tamil people, who voted for a non-racist political culture, have been let down by the government’s illegal reinstallation of the statue in Trincomalee. The union questioned why the law is not applied to those who improperly installed the statue, disturbed public order, and assaulted the police. They warned they would not accept land grabs masked as religious worship, concluding that the state’s entire machinery is now subservient to the Buddhist clergy.

“Is placing Buddha’s statues in the premises of Lord Shiva an act of reconciliation?” – Premachandran questions

Virakesari

Eelam People’s Revolutionary Liberation Front (EPRLF) Leader and Democratic Tamil National Alliance (DTNA) Spokesperson Suresh Premachandran stated that the illegal construction of new Buddha statues and ‘Buddhisation’ activities in Trincomalee, particularly around the Koneswaram Temple area, undermine its historical significance and the religious sentiments of Tamils, and will not contribute to national development or ethnic unity. He noted that successive Sinhala governments have surrounded the Koneswaram Temple—a site referred to in the Devaarams (Hindu devotional poems) and over 2,500 years old—with Buddha statues and Buddhist institutions. Premachandran further pointed out that areas traditionally used by Tamils for last rites, such as the Kannya Hot Water Wells, have been encroached upon by Buddhist monks who now manage the site and are demolishing Hindu temples there.

“Do not release land held by the military; national security is crucial for the country” – SJB MP Nalin Bandara insists in Parliament

Thinakkural, Thamilan

SJB MP Nalin Bandara urged the government not to release lands linked to national security in the Northern Province, emphasising that national security must take precedence over individual security in decisions regarding land release. Speaking in Parliament yesterday (18th), he stated that the thirty-year war is not truly over and that future conflicts could occur, making it necessary for the military to retain strategically important land. Bandara accused the government of neglecting national security because of election promises made in the North. He suggested that rather than releasing security-related land, the government should provide alternative land or fair compensation to rightful owners.

“No ban on commemorating relatives; if LTTE is commemorated, legal action will be taken” – Nalinda Jayatissa

Virakesari

Cabinet Spokesperson Nalinda Jayatissa stated that there are no restrictions on citizens commemorating relatives who died in the country’s thirty-year civil war, and every citizen has the right to do so. However, he emphasised that if terrorists are commemorated on this basis, legal action will be taken against those involved. Minister Jayatissa also stated that since the current government assumed office in November last year, various groups have tried to incite racism, but these efforts have failed. He confirmed that the government intends to prevent any recurrence of such incidents and will take all necessary steps to promote ethnic unity and reconciliation, ensuring peace and order in the country.

SINHALA PRESS PRIORITIES
Photo credits: Sri Lanka Mirror

NPP secures control of the Seethawaka Pradeshiya Sabha six months after the election

Lankadeepa, Divaina, Mawbima, Dinamina

Six months after the local government (LG) election, the NPP established control over the Seethawaka Pradeshiya Sabha yesterday (18th). Consequently, P.K. Premarathna, a member of the NPP, was appointed as chairperson of the Seethawaka Pradeshiya Sabha. In the previous LG election, no party or independent group managed to secure more than 50% of the required seats to assume control of the Seethawaka Pradeshiya Sabha. The NPP won 23 seats, while the remaining 24 seats were distributed among opposition parties. Therefore, the Seethawaka Pradeshiya Sabha, which had not been able to form an administration even after six months, reconvened yesterday (18th). An initial decision was made on whether the vote to elect the chairperson would be an open or secret ballot. The 23 members of the NPP and one member representing Sarvajana Balaya from the opposition opted for a secret ballot. Subsequently, a vote was held and NPP's Premarathna was elected chairperson of the Seethawaka Pradeshiya Sabha.

“None of the government bungalows have been given to MPs or ministers” – Nalinda Jayatissa

Dinamina

Cabinet Spokesperson Minister Nalinda Jayatissa stated that no government bungalows or houses have been given to any MP, minister, or politician. The minister made this statement yesterday (18th) at the media briefing on Cabinet decisions at the Department of Government Information. Jayatissa added that of the thirty-two government bungalows, six have been temporarily leased: four to the Ministry of Justice, one to the Police Department, and one to the Commission to Investigate Allegations of Bribery or Corruption (CIABOC).

A programme from the government to solve the problems of local potato farmers

Dinamina

The government plans to introduce a systematic programme, with short-term, medium-term, and long-term measures, to resolve issues faced by local potato farmers. As part of this programme, focus has been placed on purchasing potatoes from farmers at a price between Rs. 200 and Rs. 300 per kilogram, establishing a purchasing mechanism with Sathosa, and increasing the tax on imported potatoes. This was discussed yesterday (18th) at the Presidential Secretariat with Minister of Agriculture, Livestock, Land and Irrigation K.D. Lalkantha, Minister of Trade, Commerce, Food Security and Cooperative Development Wasantha Samarasinghe, and local potato farmers.

“I will not speak in Parliament for one month if 20,000 people do not attend the Nugegoda rally” – MP Chamara Sampath

Lankadeepa

New Democratic Front (NDF) MP Chamara Sampath declared that he will not speak in Parliament for one month if 20,000 people do not attend the anti-government rally scheduled for November 21. The rally is being organised by the united opposition against the government. Sampath made this statement in Parliament yesterday (18th).

Trincomalee statue incident: president requests a report from the public security secretary | “No room for racism; future murder investigations such as Thajudeen’s will be handled accurately” – President

Lankadeepa, Divaina, Mawbima, Dinamina, Aruna, Virakesari, Thinakaran, Thinakkural, Thamilan

President Anura Kumara Dissanayake told Parliament yesterday (18th) that he has instructed the Secretary to the Ministry of Public Security to submit a report on the incident at the Sri Sambuddha Jayanthi Bodhiraja Viharaya in Trincomalee. President Dissanayake stated that the drug trade and criminal gangs will be eliminated, and pledged to build a clean state for future generations by overcoming all organised conspiracies. He added that investigations into crimes such as the death of Thajudeen and the Easter Sunday attack will in future be conducted accurately. The president asserted that the aim of the defeated forces is to provoke racism and stressed that he would not permit racism to resurface. He made these remarks during the debate on the expenditure heads of the Ministry of Defence and the Ministry of Public Security and Parliamentary Affairs.

Draft approved: bill to be tabled in Parliament soon; pensions of 330 former MPs cancelled

Lankadeepa, Divaina, Mawbima, Dinamina, Virakesari, Thinakaran

Cabinet approval has been granted to submit the Parliamentary Pensions Law (Repeal) Bill for parliamentary approval. Cabinet Spokesperson Minister Nalinda Jayatissa stated that after this bill is enacted, no MP will be entitled to a pension in future. The minister made this statement yesterday (18th) during the media briefing on Cabinet decisions at the Department of Government Information.
