"Government attempts to establish 14 wind turbines in Mannar; the NPP must be accountable" – MP Shanakiyan urges

Virakesari, Thinakkural

ITAK MP Shanakiyan Rasamanickam, speaking in Parliament yesterday (20th) during the committee stage debate on the 2026 Budget, highlighted a contradiction in the president’s position regarding wind power in Mannar. Shanakiyan pointed out that the president, who had promised during the presidential election that no new wind power plants would be allowed in Mannar, is now insisting on constructing 14 wind turbines there against the wishes of the local population. He asserted that it is incorrect to claim the people have given their consent for the construction of these wind turbines and noted the distress caused to residents. Consequently, he called for NPP MPs representing the Northern Province to be held responsible for this issue. He noted that the ministry previously responsible for the collapse of the government is now under Minister Kumara Jayakody. This ministry was the most affected during the economic crisis in 2022 and 2023. The programmes undertaken by this ministry led to widespread protests in the country.

14 salons operated by the army in the North; Minister Bimal's statement proven false

Thinakkural

The Hairdressers Federation Association Northern Province rejected Minister Bimal Rathnayake’s recent parliamentary statement that the military operates only one hair salon in the Northern Province. In a media release, the association countered that the army runs 14 hair salons across five districts, detailing locations near Army, Special Task Force, and Civil Defence camps in Jaffna, Vavuniya, Kilinochchi, Mullaitivu, and Mannar. The Federation stated that this commercial activity is severely affecting the livelihoods of its members and breaches their regulations, demanding the immediate closure of these army-run salons.

"Do not antagonise India" – MP Ajith Perera urges

Thinakkural, Thamilan

SJB MP Ajith P. Perera emphasised that Sri Lanka should not antagonise India and must work cooperatively to develop the remaining 66 tanks in the Trincomalee oil tank farm. He stressed that the government should not pursue an anti-India policy. Speaking in Parliament yesterday (20th), he maintained that insulating Sri Lanka’s economic future from India is unsustainable, and the government must work harmoniously with India across all sectors. He called for both countries to operate on a mutually beneficial policy.

"We will strive to secure justice for Tamils" – UK All-Party Parliamentary Group affirms | "Cooperation to guarantee religious freedom" – UK Parliamentary Secretary announces

Virakesari

A meeting was held between representatives of various British parliamentary parties and the British Tamil Forum (BTF) to discuss key issues faced by Sri Lankan Tamils, focusing on accountability, justice, and sustainable peace. The topics covered included establishing a DNA data bank, seeking support from the ICC or other international investigative mechanisms, and reviewing the political direction of the current NPP government. Members of the All-Party Parliamentary Group (APPG) reaffirmed their strong commitment to attaining accountability, securing justice for the Tamil people, and supporting human rights and lasting peace. UK Parliamentary Secretary for Religious Freedom, Seema Malhotra, announced in the UK Parliament that Britain will continue to cooperate with Sri Lanka to safeguard religious freedom and the rights of all communities. She emphasised that the UK consistently stresses the need to protect the right of all Sri Lankans to practise their religion without oppression and highlighted the importance of religious tolerance.

Secretary to the Ministry of Education clarifies school hours extension

Thinakaran

The Ministry of Education has refuted allegations made by teacher trade unions regarding a lack of consultation on education reforms, including the proposal to extend school hours until 2.00 pm from next January. The Ministry stated that unions had multiple opportunities to share their views, but some organisations chose to boycott the discussions. Secretary to the ministry, Nalaka Kaluwewa, explained that although all unions were invited on several occasions, some representatives refused to participate, stressing that this was not a failure of the Ministry. He further clarified that holding individual discussions with over 200,000 teachers is not practical, but the reforms have already been discussed with a significant number of teachers at the provincial and zonal levels.

SINHALA PRESS PRIORITIES
Photo credits: The Island

“The SJB’s battle against the government will begin in January” – MP Hector Appuhamy

Divaina, Dinamina

SJB MP Hector Appuhamy announced that the SJB has decided not to participate in the rally organised by several opposition political parties, headed by the SLPP, scheduled to be held in Nugegoda today (21st). He also stated that this decision was not intended as a slight against other opposition parties or to undermine the rally. According to Divaina, Appuhamy also indicated that the SJB’s campaign against the government will begin in January 2026. He made these remarks during a press conference yesterday (20th) at the main SJB office.

“No change to the official uniform of nurses” – Minister of Health Nalinda Jayatissa

Mawbima, Dinamina

Minister of Health Dr. Nalinda Jayatissa confirmed in Parliament yesterday (20th) that the government has not yet made any decision regarding changes to the nurses’ official uniform. The minister stated that MP Hizbullah raised this matter as a private member’s motion to Parliament 33 years ago and that if a change is to be made, suggestions and proposals should be submitted to the Uniform Committee chaired by the Director General of Health Services.

“A situation in which a large number of FR petitions are being rejected has arisen” – MP Ajith P. Perera

Anidda

SJB MP Ajith P. Perera stated that recently there has been a trend of Fundamental Rights (FR) petitions being rejected in significant numbers. He added that there is also a growing pattern of certain judges not being appointed to hear these FR petitions. The MP made these remarks on Monday (17th) during the parliamentary debate on the expenditure for the Ministry of Justice.

“No solutions from the president for the issues” – MP Shanakiyan Rasamanickam

Anidda

ITAK MP Shanakiyan Rasamanickam stated that the president gave only vague responses to issues raised on behalf of the Tamil community during the meeting with ITAK MPs at the Presidential Secretariat on Wednesday (19th). Rasamanickam emphasised that the president provided no definitive answers. He said that the meeting between the ITAK and the president was prompted by a letter the ITAK sent in July 2025 requesting political solutions for the Tamil people in the North and East under a new constitution.

Ratama Ekata national mission commences in the South: “We will destroy the apex of the toxic drug network” – President states in Tangalle

Lankadeepa, Divaina, Mawbima, Dinamina, Virakesari, Thinakaran, Thinakkural, Thamilan

President Anura Kumara Dissanayake stressed that armed criminals and those connected to the drug network have become powerful due to political patronage, and that no criminal or anyone linked to the drug trade will be granted political protection by the current government. He also noted that at one time, certain kingpins of the underworld were part of political party executive committees and even represented the Presidential Security Division. Asserting the government’s determination to eradicate drugs from the country, President Dissanayake said that a family, village, town, and nation free of drugs must be created. He added that the mission to eliminate drugs would be carried out with firm commitment and the nation would certainly be rescued from the drug scourge by dismantling the apex of the drug network. The president further pointed out that politicians who have profited through the drug trade are now spending significant amounts organising rallies, protests, and other events. He made these comments while attending the 'Ratama Ekata' National Mission programme held at Tangalle Public Stadium yesterday (20th) afternoon, with the aim of freeing Sri Lankan society from the scourge of drugs.

The united opposition’s rally in Nugegoda to be held today (21st)

Lankadeepa, Divaina, Mawbima, Dinamina, Virakesari, Thinakaran, Thinakkural, Thamilan

General Secretary of the SLPP, MP Sagara Kariyawasam, announced that the 'Maha Jana Handa' rally, organised by several opposition parties against the government, will take place today (21st) at the Ananda Samarakoon Open-Air Stage in Nugegoda, with the participation of leaders from 17 political parties and organisations. Kariyawasam added that the rally will begin at 1.00 pm with opposition leaders and members in attendance, and a large crowd is expected. Leaders and representatives from numerous parties, including the SLPP, Sarvajana Balaya Party (SBP), SLFP, and Pivithuru Hela Urumaya (PHU), are scheduled to participate today (21st).
