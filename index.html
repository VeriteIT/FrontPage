TAMIL PRESS PRIORITIES
Photo credits:

“Allocate at least Rs.1.1 billion to conduct the election” – Weerawansa

Thamilan, Thinakkural

Freedom People’s Alliance (FPA) Leader Wimal Weerawansa stated that the president cannot put on a show in parliament and should confine his actions to the secretariat. He further argued that the government must release Rs.1.1 billion, as claimed by the Election Commission, to conduct the election, or significant public objections and consequences could arise, as seen in previous incidents.

Sri Lankan High Commission surrounded in Chennai

Thinakkural

The ‘May 17’ Movement in Tamil Nadu organised a protest in front of the Sri Lankan High Commission, condemning the recent severe attacks within two weeks by the Sri Lanka Navy (SLN) on Indian fisherfolk from Tharangambadi and Karaikal. Thirumurugan Gandhi, coordinator of the ‘May 17’ Movement, asserted that Sri Lanka must stop the violence against Tamil Nadu fisherfolk.

“Ten new ministers to be sworn in” – Dissanayake

Thinakkural, Thamilan

SLPP MP S.B. Dissanayake stated that President Wickremesinghe will soon appoint ten new ministers, including himself. He added that Gamini Lokuge, Janaka Bandara Thennakoon, Johnston Fernando, S.M. Chandrasena, C.B. Rathnayake, Rohitha Abegunawardhana and Sarath Weerasekera are among those to be sworn in as new ministers.

Political interference must not occur – HRCSL tells president

Virakesari

The Human Rights Commission of Sri Lanka (HRCSL) has recommended that the president ensure the independence of the Truth and Reconciliation Commission (TRC), which is to be established by an act of parliament. The recommendation states that the TRC must be empowered to uncover the truth, promote reconciliation, and establish peace and justice, free from political interference.

SINHALA PRESS PRIORITIES
Photo credits:

CCTV cameras mandatory for school vans

Dinamina, Aruna

Minister of Transport, Highways, and Urban Development Bimal Rathnayake stated yesterday (24th) in parliament that the installation of CCTV cameras in school vans will be mandatory from next January. Rathnayake made this announcement during the debate on the ministry’s budget allocations. He added that this measure will enable parents to monitor their children from home. Rathnayake further stated: "A set of instructions will be issued for school vans and office vans starting in January. Passengers must be encouraged to report any harassment of women or children in public transport. Failing to speak up if one witnesses wrongdoing is not acceptable. Turning a blind eye when misconduct occurs is wrong." He also said steps will be taken to elevate the bus and three-wheeler industries to a professional standard.

22,000 kg of drugs seized in 11 months; 26,573 arrests during Operation Ratama Ekata in 25 days

Dinamina

Within 25 days of launching the "Ratama Ekata" (A Nation United) operation on October 30, 26,573 people have been arrested in raids, uncovering nearly 1,850 kg of illicit drugs. The largest quantity was found during raids at sea. The Sri Lanka Navy (SLN) conducted the raids based on information from the Police Narcotics Bureau (PNB), State Intelligence Service, and the Navy Intelligence Unit. This is reported to be the largest drug seizure in the shortest time in the country’s history. So far this year, 221,246 people have been arrested from January to date and 22,056 kg of drugs seized.

UNP and SJB ready for alliance talks

Lankadeepa

General Secretary of the SJB, Ranjith Madduma Bandara, stated that SJB Leader Sajith Premadasa has agreed to work with the UNP. He said high-level talks between the two parties are scheduled soon. He stressed that all collaboration between the parties will take place with the knowledge of the SJB leader.

COMMON PRIORITIES: SINHALA & TAMIL PRESS

Six buried alive: landslide strikes area under warning; four hospitalised

Lankadeepa, Divaina, Mawbima, Dinamina, Aruna, Virakesari, Thinakaran, Thinakkural, Thamilan

On Saturday (22nd) morning, a landslide involving a large rock struck a house and a shop in Lower Kadugannawa on the Colombo–Kandy main road. The death toll from the disaster at around 8.45 am has risen to six. Four bodies were immediately recovered; two more, who had been trapped under debris, were found yesterday (23rd). Five injured people have been admitted to hospital. The incident occurred when a large rock and several smaller rocks below the railway line fell onto the house and shop. A joint rescue effort by the police, army, local residents, and health officials was launched to free those trapped inside. A section of the Colombo–Kandy main road remains closed after the landslide. Reports indicate that warnings had been issued to the occupants regarding dangerous weather conditions.

“Politically influential people in Batticaloa remove signboards at archaeological sites: legal action to follow” – Director General of Archaeology

Mawbima, Aruna, Thinakkural, Thamilan

Police report that an investigation began following complaints made yesterday (23rd) about the removal of name boards at five archaeological sites in the Manmunai South and Koralaipattu Valaichchenai Divisional Secretariat areas of Batticaloa. The boards, listing the archaeological names Thandamalai (Rahat Gala), Murunkantivu, Thikkilivettai, Pendukkal Chenai, and Saraveli, were removed, loaded onto tractors, and taken away. Acting Director General of the Department of Archaeology Thusitha Ranasinghe stated that complaints had been lodged and that legal action would be taken. Archaeological signboards, erected by the Department of Archaeology in villages under the Koralaipattu Divisional Council, were removed on November 22 by a group including the Divisional Council chairperson, councillors, and the public. The boards had been placed at junctions on roads passing through areas such as Kudumbimalai and its surrounding hills, ancient temples, and farmlands in Kiran. Divisional Council chairperson S. Sudhakaran argued that the signboards were installed without council approval and described the move as “an act of betrayal against an ethnic group” for failing to consider the local language. He further claimed the signs were meant to bring the sites under the future control of the Department of Archaeology.
