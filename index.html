"Tamil people resent the Ministry of Defence, which is responsible for all their suffering" – Gajendrakumar Ponnambalam

Thinakkural, Virakesari, Thamilan

All Ceylon Tamil Congress (ACTC) MP Gajendrakumar Ponnambalam asserted that Tamil people deeply resent the Ministry of Defence, which he claimed is responsible for all the suffering experienced by Tamils both during and after the civil war. Speaking in parliament yesterday (18th), he alleged that military personnel in the North and East are involved in drug distribution, noting that the drug problem worsened after the war, unlike during the LTTE administration. Ponnambalam demanded the government recognise that war crimes and human rights violations occurred during the war, citing evidence released by individuals with integrity within the military. He insisted that as the government and the military face such accusations, they must agree to international investigations to regain the trust of the Tamil people.

"Will Tamils ever get justice in Sri Lanka? The true face of the Anura government exposed today; Buddhist monks act as the country's executive presidents" – Jaffna University Students' Union strongly condemns

Virakesari, Thinakkural

Representatives of the Jaffna University Student Union argued that the root of the ethnic problem and war is Sinhala Buddhist majoritarianism, under which Tamil lands have continually been subjected to Buddhist-Sinhala dominance in the name of Buddha worship. Speaking at a media briefing yesterday (18th), they said Tamil people, who had hoped for a non-racist political culture by voting, have been completely betrayed by the government through the illegal reinstallation of the statue in Trincomalee. The union questioned why the law is not enforced against those responsible for illegally installing the statue, disturbing the peace, and assaulting the police. They warned that they would not accept systematic land grabbing under the pretext of worship, concluding that the state mechanism is entirely subservient to the monks.

“Is placing Buddha statues on the premises of Lord Shiva acts of reconciliation?” – Premachandran raises the question

Virakesari

Eelam People’s Revolutionary Liberation Front (EPRLF) Leader and Democratic Tamil National Alliance (DTNA) Spokesperson Suresh Premachandran stated that the illegal erection of new Buddha statues and Buddhisation activities in the Trincomalee and Koneswaram Temple area, which undermine its historical significance and the religious sentiments of Tamils, will never help the development or ethnic unity of the country. He said successive Sinhala governments have surrounded the base of the Koneswaram Temple, a sacred site mentioned in the Devaarams (Hindu devotional poems) and over 2,500 years old, with Buddha statues and Buddhist institutions. Premachandran noted that areas traditionally used by Tamils for last rites, such as the Kannya Hot Water Wells, have been seized by Buddhist monks who now administer the site and are demolishing Hindu temples there.

“Do not release land held by the military! National security is vital for the country” – SJB MP Nalin Bandara insists in parliament

Thinakkural, Thamilan

SJB MP Nalin Bandara urged the government not to release lands linked to national security in the Northern Province, stressing that national security must take precedence over individual safety in making decisions about the release of such land. Speaking in parliament yesterday (18th), he argued that the thirty-year war is not fully over and that future conflicts could arise, so strategically important lands must remain with the military. Bandara accused the government of acting irresponsibly and neglecting national security for political gain in the North. He proposed that instead of releasing security-related lands, the government should provide alternative land or appropriate compensation to the rightful owners.

“No ban on commemorating relatives; if the LTTE is commemorated, legal action will be taken” – Nalinda Jayatissa

Virakesari

Cabinet Spokesperson Nalinda Jayatissa stated there is no restriction on citizens commemorating their relatives who died during the civil war, and every citizen has the right to do so. However, he emphasised that if terrorists are commemorated under this pretext, legal action will be taken against those involved. Minister Jayatissa noted that since the current government took office in November last year, various groups have attempted to incite racism, but these efforts have been unsuccessful. He confirmed that the government is committed to preventing a recurrence of such situations and will take all necessary steps to foster ethnic unity and reconciliation, ensuring peace and order in the country.

SINHALA PRESS PRIORITIES
Photo credits: Sri Lanka Mirror

NPP secures control of the Seethawaka Pradeshiya Sabha six months after the election

Lankadeepa, Divaina, Mawbima, Dinamina

Six months after the Local Government (LG) Election, the NPP has gained control of the Seethawaka Pradeshiya Sabha as of yesterday (18th). Accordingly, P.K. Premarathna, a member of the NPP, was appointed Chairperson of the Seethawaka Pradeshiya Sabha. In the previous LG Election, no party or independent group achieved more than 50% of the seats needed for control of the Seethawaka Pradeshiya Sabha. The NPP took 23 seats, while the remaining 24 were divided among opposition parties. As a result, power had not been established for six months following the LG Election, and the Pradeshiya Sabha reconvened yesterday (18th). Firstly, it was decided whether to elect the chairperson by open or secret ballot. The 23 NPP members and one Sarvajana Balaya opposition member opted for a secret ballot. Consequently, a vote was held and NPP’s Premarathna was appointed Chairperson of the Seethawaka Pradeshiya Sabha.

“None of the government bungalows have been allocated to MPs or ministers” – Nalinda Jayatissa

Dinamina

Cabinet Spokesperson Minister Nalinda Jayatissa stated that no government bungalows or houses have been allocated to any MP, minister, or politician. He made this statement yesterday (18th) at the Cabinet decisions media briefing held at the Department of Government Information. Jayatissa added that out of 32 government bungalows, six have been temporarily leased: four to the Ministry of Justice, one to the Police Department, and one to the Commission to Investigate Allegations of Bribery or Corruption (CIABOC).

A programme from the government to resolve the problems of local potato farmers

Dinamina

The government plans to formulate a systematic programme through short-, medium-, and long-term measures to address the issues faced by local potato farmers. This programme aims to purchase potatoes from farmers at a price between Rs. 200 and Rs. 300 per kilogram, set up a mechanism to buy potatoes in collaboration with Sathosa, and increase the tax on imported potatoes. A discussion on the matter took place yesterday (18th) at the Presidential Secretariat with Minister of Agriculture, Livestock, Land and Irrigation K.D. Lalkantha, Minister of Trade, Commerce, Food Security and Cooperative Development Wasantha Samarasinghe, and potato farmers.

“I will not speak in parliament for a month if 20,000 people do not attend the Nugegoda rally” – MP Chamara Sampath

Lankadeepa

New Democratic Front (NDF) MP Chamara Sampath declared that he will not speak in parliament for a month if 20,000 people do not attend the anti-government rally scheduled for November 21. The rally is being organised by the united opposition against the government. Sampath made this statement in parliament yesterday (18th).

Trincomalee statue incident: President requests report from Public Security Secretary | “No tolerance for racism: murder investigations such as Thajudeen’s will be conducted accurately in future” – President

Lankadeepa, Divaina, Mawbima, Dinamina, Aruna, Virakesari, Thinakaran, Thinakkural, Thamilan

President Anura Kumara Dissanayake stated in parliament yesterday (18th) that he has instructed the Secretary to the Ministry of Public Security to submit a report on the incident at the Sri Sambuddha Jayanthi Bodhiraja Viharaya in Trincomalee. The President also said the drug trade and criminal gangs will be completely eradicated, and that he is determined to build a clean state for future generations by defeating all organised conspiracies. He added that investigations into cases like Thajudeen’s death and the Easter Sunday attack will be thoroughly conducted in the future. The President emphasised that the aim of defeated forces is to create a climate of racism and insisted that he would not allow racism to re-emerge. The President made these remarks during a debate on the expenditure heads of the Ministry of Defence and the Ministry of Public Security and Parliamentary Affairs.

Draft approved: Act to be tabled in parliament soon; pensions of 330 former MPs cancelled

Lankadeepa, Divaina, Mawbima, Dinamina, Virakesari, Thinakaran

Cabinet approval has been granted to submit the Parliamentary Pensions Law (Repeal) Bill for parliamentary approval. Cabinet Spokesperson Minister Nalinda Jayatissa stated that after the Bill is enacted, no MP will receive a pension in future. The Minister made this announcement yesterday (18th) during the Cabinet decisions media briefing at the Department of Government Information.
